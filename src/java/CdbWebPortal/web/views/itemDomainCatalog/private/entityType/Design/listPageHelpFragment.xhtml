Design:

  • By definition, it is a plan, drawing, or blueprint produced to show the look and function or workings of a building, group of components and designs, or other object before it is built or made
  • It can be found in the Design Library
  • A design may have a state of “work in progress” or "completed"*
    • Modifications to a completed design could be done by cloning it and assigning cloned design a new revision*
  • It may represent a template for a specific design implementation or a blueprint for an assembly component (“assembly design”)*

Assembly Design:*

  • Once completed “assembly designs” will become un-modifiable assembly components that will be created in the Component Catalog and linked to the originating design via assembly’s component property*

Template Design:

  • Can be used as templates for more specific design implementations (i.e., they can have design instances)*
  • May have optional design elements that may or may not be implemented by design instances*
*Design states, design cloning, design assemblies, and design implementations(instances) are coming in the next major release of CDB.
